Acting & Improvisation

DOE course number: 52.06100


Grades: 10-12


One elective credit


One weekly 60-min class

Need to de-stress after a long week of classes? Join us for improv and scene study that will stretch your observation, concentration, and imagination!

Objectives:

  1. Sharpen observation and reaction skills by playing theatre games and participating in warm up activities.
  2. Study and practice Robert Cohen's foundational acting concepts of victory, obstacle, and tactics.
  3. Develop scenes based on spontaneous situations
  4. Interact with content-less scenes as well as scenes from traditional theatre.
  5. Investigate common theatre practices such as blocking movement, eating stage food, fighting, and staying in character.
  6. Strengthen skills that encourage others and build a safe acting space.
  7. Learn cooperation, leadership, and organizational techniques.
  8. Attend at least one educational or professional production per semester.
  9. Read at least one complete play script per semester outside of class and discuss it in class.
  10. Understand use of a contract for fulfilling course objectives and creating a grade.
